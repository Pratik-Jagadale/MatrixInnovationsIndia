Deepotsav (दीपोत्सव)


Celebration of Lights and victory of good over evil and darkness - is the ultimate definition of the ancient Hindu festival - “Deepavali” (दीपावली), also known as “Deepotsav” (दीपोत्सव).

The word “Deepotsav” (दीपोत्सव) is a combination of two words :- “Deep” (दीप) and “Utsav” (उत्सव). “Deep” (दीप) is the Sanskrit word for ‘a flaming light’, and “Utsav” (उत्सव) means ‘celebration’.

The festivities of “Deepotsav” begin with putting up lights in temples, followed by offering of lights and prayers to the Gods. Afterward, the celebration takes on a grander form - with diyas, fire-crackers, rangoli, distribution of sweets among friends and family and offering of sweets to the Gods, etc. and then the celebration ends - yet again - in prayers and the traditional ritual of the “Lakshmi Poojan”.
